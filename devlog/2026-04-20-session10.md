## What was built

### All 30 synperson event diaries

Completed the full 30-person synperson panel event histories. Every synperson now has a `events.md` file with 15 seed events covering their narrative life from first career moment through April 2026, following the exponential-decay density distribution defined in `_events_schema.md`.

**Files created:** `synpersons/{30 directories}/events.md` — one per synperson, all 30 complete.

**Storytelling rules established:** `synpersons/_storytelling_rules.md` (created this session) — 11 rules governing voice, emotional register, event selection, duration-to-sentiment mapping, and temporal structure. Key rules:

1. Milestone ambivalence — every significant event has mixed feelings
2. 80/20 personal-to-public ratio — ~20% of events reference real historical events (COVID, 9/11, 2016 election, Brexit, financial crisis, etc.)
3. Durations not instants — events have start and end dates; narrative boundary points, not timestamps
4. Full duration spectrum — days to years; choose the duration that matches the emotional arc
5. Duration–emotion rule — longer duration → chronic register; shorter → acute
6. Fuzzy boundaries allowed — "Fall 2011", "Christmas week", "May or June" are all valid
7. Private regret over public narrative — the interior experience is the story
8. No retrospective wisdom — people in the diary don't know what they'll know later
9. Public events from the inside out — 9/11 is felt from a specific position, not narrated as history
10. Overlapping intervals — events can run concurrently (Allen's interval calculus noted as the planned formal model)
11. At least one uncomplicatedly good event per person

**`_events_schema.md` updated:** date format changed from `YYYY-MM` to `YYYY-MM → YYYY-MM`; cross-reference to storytelling rules added.

### Archetype-consistent event patterns

Events were calibrated to the six archetypes rather than being generic life events:

- **Contrarians**: public intellectual conflicts, betrayals, wrong calls, vindications, adversarial collaborations
- **Hackers**: unauthorised builds, institutional confrontations over disclosure, legal/IP tension, forks and licensing disputes
- **Holy Warriors**: sacrificial stands, institutional resistance, moral clarity at personal cost
- **Investigators**: evidence trails, escalating documentation, pattern recognition, the cost of knowing
- **Legalists**: procedural crises, the gap between correct-process and correct-outcome, long-game documentation
- **Operators**: quiet execution under constraint, invisible credit, managing political principals, chronic low-grade tension between what works and what can be said
