### Track D analysis — question-weight alignment

Derived the mathematically-defined behavioral profile for each archetype by exhaustively computing ideal answer choices for all 28 questions. Key findings:

**E/V axis — the most surprising behavioral signatures:**

| Question | Exit answer | Voice answer | What it really means |
|---|---|---|---|
| Q001 | restless (b) | angry (a) | Exit = boredom-driven; Voice = morally outraged |
| Q009 | boredom motivates (b) | anger motivates (a) | Same split repeated |
| Q013 | first principles (b) | analogies/stories (a) | Exit = analytical; Voice = narrative |
| Q015 | direction first, then tribe (b) | tribe first (a) | Exit = mission-first; Voice = community-first |
| Q019 | trust the process (a) | trust the leader (b) | Surprising: Legalists trust process, HW trusts person |
| Q021 | leave the project (b) | clean up the toxic people (a) | Exit LITERALLY EXITS; Voice STAYS AND FIGHTS |
| Q022 | principles over friends (b) | loyalty to friends (a) | Exit = abstract-principled; Voice = relational |

Q021 is the definitional question: Exit archetypes literally leave organizations when politics turns toxic. They don't fight to clean it up — that's Voice.

**Ethics dimension — what the labels actually encode in this test (not classical philosophy):**

- **Virtue** (Legalist, Holy Warrior): learns from exemplars; reflective before acting; dislikes political maneuvering; thinks about lessons for future generations; seeks masters; open/accessible personality
- **Consequentialist** (Contrarian, Operator): ends justify means; analyzes carefully before deciding; motivated by what's exciting/worthwhile; enjoys political maneuvering; present-focused (Keynes quote); works on problems that make a difference
- **Deontological** (Hacker, Investigator): ends don't justify means; dives in hands-on; recognizes concrete first steps; plays to own strengths; long-term principled thinking; self-challenges; "need to know where I'm coming from" (inscrutable)

Note: "deontological" in this test doesn't mean rule-following bureaucracy. It means principled hands-on action, self-reliance, and personal moral compass. The closest real-world analogue is the craftsman's ethic or the Stoic's.

**Critical misalignments found in current narrative descriptions:**

1. **Investigator**: described as "likes clear rules and abiding by them" — but the deontological dimension encodes hands-on action, self-reliance, playing to strengths. Rule-following is actually virtue ethics (Legalist). Claude simulating a rule-following Investigator will answer like a Legalist.

2. **Holy Warrior**: "has no patience for rules and systems" — but virtue ethics means learning from exemplars and following traditional models of goodness. Holy Warriors respect the tradition; they have no patience for *corruption* of the tradition, not for rules per se.

3. **Hacker**: described as "habit-oriented" — but deontological + exit means: ends don't justify means, dives in hands-on, self-reliant, concrete first steps, challenges self. "Habit-oriented" undersells the principled integrity component.

4. **Legalist**: "enjoy aligning strategies and systems, going from reacting to chaos to proactively creating order" — this sounds like staying and fixing (Voice). Legalists *exit*. They trust the process, not the people. They don't stay to fix — they leave and build new systems elsewhere.

5. **Operator**: "detest by-the-book people and navigate by your own sense of right and wrong" — but consequentialists enjoy political maneuvering and calculate means to ends. "Own sense of right and wrong" sounds deontological/virtue, not consequentialist.

### Improvement plan defined (Tracks A–D)

Four-track plan to improve archetype hit rate from ~20% to a meaningful level (target: ≥50%):

**Track A — Memory model tuning:**
- Reduce `intensity_floor` 8 → 6 (more events pinned to active memory)
- Reduce `alpha` 0.5 → 0.2 (cast wider net on salient events)
- Raise `max_events` 10 → 12
- Remove 4-relationship cap in prompt (include all relationships)

**Track B — Behavioral anchor field:**
- Add `behavioral_stance` to `_rig_schema.yaml` and all 30 rig.yaml files
- Text block that directly states the archetype's answer-relevant behavioral signatures
- Prompt redesign: surface `behavioral_stance` first, before event summary

**Track C — Events response beats:**
- Audit all 30 events.md files for events that encode the WRONG archetype's behavioral responses
- Fix: E/V axis events should show the correct response pattern (Exit = leave/disengage; Voice = stay/fight)
- Add explicit "response beat" lines to events showing the archetype's characteristic reaction

**Track D — Archetype description alignment (this session):**
- Analysis complete (above). Next: rewrite `behavioral_stance` text blocks for each archetype using the mathematically-derived behavioral signatures, not the narrative marketing descriptions

## Key decisions

- **Track D precedes B and C**: the behavioral anchor text (Track B) must be written with the corrected understanding (Track D) to be useful. Writing B without D would just codify the existing misalignments.
- **Do not rewrite the archetype HTML pages yet**: the narrative descriptions are for human readers and have a distinct purpose (marketing/positioning). The behavioral anchors live in rig.yaml and prompts — a separate, internal layer.
- **20% is close to random**: the current system proves the pipeline works end-to-end but does not yet encode the archetypes with enough signal. The improvement plan addresses this systematically.
