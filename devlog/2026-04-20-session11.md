Q021 is the definitional question: Exit archetypes literally leave organizations when politics turns toxic. They don't fight to clean it up — that's Voice.

**Ethics dimension — what the labels actually encode in this test (not classical philosophy):**

- **Virtue** (Legalist, Holy Warrior): learns from exemplars; reflective before acting; dislikes political maneuvering; thinks about lessons for future generations; seeks masters; open/accessible personality
- **Consequentialist** (Contrarian, Operator): ends justify means; analyzes carefully before deciding; motivated by what's exciting/worthwhile; enjoys political maneuvering; present-focused (Keynes quote); works on problems that make a difference
- **Deontological** (Hacker, Investigator): ends don't justify means; dives in hands-on; recognizes concrete first steps; plays to own strengths; long-term principled thinking; self-challenges; "need to know where I'm coming from" (inscrutable)

Note: "deontological" in this test doesn't mean rule-following bureaucracy. It means principled hands-on action, self-reliance, and personal moral compass. The closest real-world analogue is the craftsman's ethic or the Stoic's.

**Critical misalignments found in current narrative descriptions:**

1. **Investigator**: described as "likes clear rules and abiding by them" — but the deontological dimension encodes hands-on action, self-reliance, playing to strengths. Rule-following is actually virtue ethics (Legalist). Claude simulating a rule-following Investigator will answer like a Legalist.

2. **Holy Warrior**: "has no patience for rules and systems" — but virtue ethics means learning from exemplars and following traditional models of goodness. Holy Warriors respect the tradition; they have no patience for *corruption* of the tradition, not for rules per se.

3. **Hacker**: described as "habit-oriented" — but deontological + exit means: ends don't justify means, dives in hands-on, self-reliant, concrete first steps, challenges self. "Habit-oriented" undersells the principled integrity component.

4. **Legalist**: "enjoy aligning strategies and systems, going from reacting to chaos to proactively creating order" — this sounds like staying and fixing (Voice). Legalists *exit*. They trust the process, not the people. They don't stay to fix — they leave and build new systems elsewhere.

5. **Operator**: "detest by-the-book people and navigate by your own sense of right and wrong" — but consequentialists enjoy political maneuvering and calculate means to ends. "Own sense of right and wrong" sounds deontological/virtue, not consequentialist.

### Theory dialogue — ethics dimension grounding

Discussion after the QA run surfaced important clarifications about what the three ethics dimensions actually mean in this model, and how they interact with E/V.

**Deontological = behavioral self-definition.** Hackers and Investigators define themselves by how they act — the right method is morally significant independent of outcomes or exemplars. The "craftsman's ethic" or Stoic's. Not rule-following bureaucracy (that's virtue, specifically the Legalist mode).

**Virtue has two modes, split by E/V:**
- **Holy Warrior (Voice + Virtue)** = *mimetic*: become good by emulating specific exemplars; reform means restoring the institution to the standard set by its best people. Points at real people.
- **Legalist (Exit + Virtue)** = *codifying*: extract principles from (possibly implicit) models and crystallize them into abstract frameworks — codes, constitutions, definitions of good/bad. Think constitution-drafters: they may draw on Locke but the output is a normative framework, not "be like Jefferson." Completes the exit cycle: Hacker subverts → Contrarian philosophizes → Legalist codifies.

**Consequentialist = goal ethics.** Moral weight is in outcomes. Contrarians and Operators evaluate actions by what they achieve. Means are subordinate to ends.

**Key decision:** these definitions differ enough from the archetype HTML page descriptions that we should maintain them as a separate internal layer (`behavioral_stance` in rig.yaml, RESEARCH_DESIGN.md) rather than rewriting the marketing copy, which serves a different purpose.

### Question design principle: implicit E/V bias

Pure-ethics questions (no E/V weight in their vectors) can still have implicit E/V bias — the tension between the two options may feel more natural or emotionally resonant to one side of the E/V axis. A C/D question framed around ruthless efficiency vs. principled craft is a Contrarian/Hacker question in Exit idiom; it doesn't cleanly test the Operator/Investigator tension.

Two-part fix: (1) acknowledge the bias by adding a small same-direction E/V component to both options' weight vectors; (2) write mirror questions that pose the same ethics distinction from the other E/V side. Pool must be balanced: equal numbers of Exit-biased and Voice-biased questions within each ethics-pair (V/C, V/D, C/D).
