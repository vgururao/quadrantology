## E001 · 2013-07 → 2014-03 · intensity: 8
**Warwick Graduation, Back to Handsworth**

I graduated with a first in labour studies and came back to Handsworth, which wasn't what most of my cohort did. My mother was working double shifts at the Queen Elizabeth Hospital. My father had been laid off from the factory the year before. ## E002 · 2015-04 → 2015-10 · intensity: 7
**The Factory Floor Injury**

I was doing a workplace walkthrough at a Birmingham auto parts plant in April — a site I'd been covering for six months — when a conveyor malfunction caught my knee. No fractures, but ligament damage that required physiotherapy for three months. I filed a formal incident report the same day, which the factory manager said was unnecessary given the minor nature of the injury. I said the collective agreement required documentation of all injuries sustained during union representative activities. He said it was the union rep's call. I said: it's the procedure. That exchange told me everything about his relationship with procedure that I needed to know, and I've been tracking the injury documentation rate at that factory ever since.

## E003 · 2016-11 → 2017-09 · intensity: 7
**Brexit, Handsworth, the Workers**

The Brexit vote in June 2016 divided the workers I represented along lines that had nothing to do with their collective bargaining interests. Members who'd voted Leave and members who'd voted Remain were in the same negotiation about the same wage dispute, and the political division was being used by management to delay the negotiation by making the union appear internally divided. I spent nine months holding the negotiation together by keeping the Brexit conversation explicitly out of the room. Adeyemi said I was managing people rather than organizing them. I said: what's the difference? He said: one is maintenance, the other is building. I've been thinking about that since.

## E004 · 2019-06 → 2020-02 · intensity: 7
**Warwick and the Grievance Cascade**

I escalated three individual grievances into a cascade — treating them as evidence of a systematic management practice rather than isolated incidents — under a provision in the collective agreement I'd been watching for eighteen months. The legal basis was sound. Sutherland challenged the consolidation at each stage and I had documentation for each stage. The cascade took eight months to reach the formal arbitration level. The arbitrator found in favor of two of the three grievances and against one, which was the correct outcome based on the evidence. Adeyemi said I'd won on principle and made enemies in practice. He asked if I'd do it again. I said yes. He said: I know. That's what worries me.

## E006 · 2021-04 → 2021-11 · intensity: 5
**My Mother's Quarter Century**

My mother marked twenty-five years as an NHS nurse in April. The hospital did nothing formal, which is standard. I organised a small family dinner in Handsworth. My aunt came from Birmingham, my uncle from Wolverhampton. My mother talked for the first time in my memory about the early years — arriving from Lagos in 1993, the nursing home positions before the NHS job, the specific colleagues who had helped her understand which rules mattered and which could bend. I listened and thought about the procedural instinct she'd given me without naming it. She'd navigated a new country using the rules as her map in exactly the way I'd grown up learning to do.

## E007 · 2022-03 → 2022-11 · intensity: 7
**Sutherland's New Contract Language**

Sutherland introduced new shift scheduling language in the 2022 contract renewal that gave management increased discretion over rotation assignments. The language was technically within the bounds of the collective agreement. I spent four months documenting how the discretion was being used before calling it a systematic practice in a formal grievance. Callum told me the grievance was correct on the facts but the timing was poor — two months before contract negotiations. I said the timing was determined by the evidence, not by the negotiation calendar. Callum said: I know that's how you think and I'm telling you what the environment says. I filed the grievance. The environment was correct.
